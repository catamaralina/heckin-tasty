IHOP Pancakes

Effort Level: Not too much.

Makes 2-4 people

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • dash salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup +2 tbsp milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice/white vinegar
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • optional add-ins: banana, apple, chocolate chip

Directions

  1. Mix milk and lemon juice to create buttermilk. Stir and set aside.
  2. Mix dry ingredients
  3. Stir egg and buttermilk mixture into dry ingredients.
  4. Mix in melted butter and sugar and any add-ins. Don't overmix - clumps are ok.
  5. Cook on med-high lightly greased pan.

Sweet Blueberry Biscuits with Lemon Glaze

Effort level: yes, 30 min

Makes about 10 biscuits

Ingredients

    Biscuits
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup butter, frozen & shaved
  • 1 1/4 cup buttermilk (2 tbsp lemon juice with 1 cup + 2 tbsp milk)
  • 1 cup frozen blueberries (or raspberries!)
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
    Glaze
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 Tbsp milk
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon zest (optional)

Directions

  1. Mix together flour, sugar, baking powder and salt.
  2. Add buttermilk and butter and add until incorportated. Do not overmix. Will be wet and sticky
  3. Dump onto floured surface. Work dough with hands until no longer sticky.
  4. Pat dough to 1/2 inch thickness. Scatter blueberries over dough. Fold dough in half. Fold dough in half again.
  5. Pat dough to 1 inch thickness. Use biscuit cutter to cut biscuits. Cut straight up and down, do not twist.
  6. Place biscuits into greased 9" pan, making sure they are touching.
  7. Preheat oven to 500° F. Bake for 8-9 minutes. Remove before they brown.
  8. Brush with melted butter then return to oven and bake until golden brown on top.
  9. GLAZE: Add all ingredients to bowl. Whisk until smooth. Drizzle over hot biscuits.

Rice Porridge (Risengrod or Risgrynsgrot)

Effort level: yeah, 1 hour

Makes 5-6.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup short-grain rice
  • 1 1/2 cup water
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4 1/2 cups milk
  • cinnamon-sugar, to taste
  • vanilla extract, to taste
  • butter, to taste

Directions

  1. Rinse rice well and drain.
  2. In saucepan bring water, butter and salt to boil over high heat.
  3. Pour in rice, stirring constantly to prevent sticking.
  4. Reduce heat to low, stirring until boiling is reduced to a simmer.
  5. Cover pot and simmer for 10-15 minues, until rice has absorbed most of the water.
  6. Add milk to rice, stirring to incorporate. Bring mixture to a boil, stirring constantly, the immediately reduce heat to low.
  7. Once at a simmer, cover pot and allow to cook for 45 minutes, without stirring.
  8. Stir in vanilla once done. Serve with cinammon-sugar, and butter. You can also pour milk over top.

Asian Noodle Soup

Effort level: some, 30 min

Makes 4

Ingredients

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 8 oz white chicken
  • 1 cup chopped bok choy
  • 2 cup spinach
  • 4 scallions, reserve dark green parts
  • 1 Tbsp minced ginger (1 1/4 tsp ground)
  • 1/4 Tbsp minced garlic (or more til your heart's content)
  • 6 vegetable boullion cubes (or chicken) (or enough or 8 c water)
  • splash of soy sauce
  • sesame seed oil
  • 8 cups water
  • salt to taste

Directions

  1. Cook noodles according to package. Wash in cold water after cooking. Stir 3-4 drops sesame oil to noodles to keep from clumping. Add green part of scallions and set aside.
  2. In large pot heat 8 cups water and boullion cubes with some soya sauce, rest of the scallions, garlic and ginger. Salt to taste. Simmer covered for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the thinkly sliced chicken. Cover and keep at gentle boil until chicken is done.
  4. Toss in bok choy and the spinach. Take off heat
  5. Put rice noodles in big soup bowls and ladle hot soup over with your favorite additions.

Asparagus Sweet Potato Chicken Skillet

Effort level: Not much , under 30 min

Makes 4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breasts (boneless)
  • olive oil
  • salt & pepper, to taste
  • 1 Tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 med sweet potato (cubed into bite-size pieces)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water
  • 1/2 lb fresh asparagus cut in 1-2 inch pieces
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper

Directions

  1. Cut chicken into small pieces, season with salt and pepper.
  2. In pan over medium heat, add Tbsp olive oil, garlic and chicken.
  3. Saute chicken for 7-10 minutes or until cooked through. Set chicken aside.
  4. In the now empty pan, add sweet potatoes and chicken broth.
  5. Cook for 7-10 minutes, or until sweet potatoes are cooked.
  6. Add asparagus and cook for 4-5 minutes until tender.
  7. Add chicken back into pan and stir to combine.
  8. Season with salt, pepper (1/2 tsp each recommended) and crushed red pepper.

Mujadara | Lebanese Lentils and Rice

Effort level: not bad - good leftovers, 20-60 min

Makes 6

Ingredients

  • 5 small onions, sliced
  • 1.5 c green or brown lentils
  • 1 c white (or brown) rice
  • 5-5 1/2 c water, divided
  • 1 Tbsp boullion powder (veggie recommended)
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt, divided
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 bay leaves
  • black pepper, to taste
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • Yogurt, herbs for garnish

Directions

  1. Soak lentils and rice in seperate bowls for about 20 min, drain.
  2. Add lentils to large pot with 3 c water, boullion powder, 1 tsp salt, black pepper and 2 bay leaves.
  3. Simmer covered for 7-10 min
  4. Add white rice with 2 c water and bring to boil.
  5. Reduce heat, cover and cook for 18-20 min.
  6. Meanwhile in hot pan with oil, add onions. Stir and reduce heat to medium and cover with lid.
  7. Cook onions for 10 min then uncover and stir thoroughly.
  8. Add cumin, 1/2 tsp salt and cook for 5-10 min uncovered.
  9. Check that lentils and rice are tender. If not, simmmer for a bit longer.
  10. When finished turn off heat, set pot aside still covered for a few minutes.
  11. Remove bay leaves, then add 2/3 of carmelized onions to lentils and rice.
  12. Serve in bowls with yogurt and rest of carmelized onions for garnish.

Sunomono | Japanese Cucumber Salad

Effort level: not much , 5 min

Makes 2

Ingredients

  • 1 cucumber (optional to peel)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 3 Tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • salt

Directions

  1. Cut cucumbers in half lengthwise then slice in moderate thick slices.
  2. Sprinkle slices with salt and sit for 5 min. Blot cucumbers with paper towel to remove excess moisture
  3. Put cucumbers in bowl. Add soy sauce, vinegar, sugar and mix well
  4. Top with sesame seeds.
  5. Optional: Make dish 1 hour before serving for best results.
  6. Variation: Grate carrots or zucchini and do similar process as cucumbers with the salt, removing excess water.

Chew Double Chocolate Cookies

Effort level: some (using scale for measuring), 15-30 min

Makes 2-3 dozen

Ingredients

  • 325 g ap flour (2 1/2 c)
  • 60 g cocoa powder (3/4 c)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 255 g unsalted butter (18 Tbsp)
  • 180 g white sugar (1 c)
  • 180 g brown sugar (1 c)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 340 g chocolate chunks (2 c)

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 350° F
  2. Cream butter and both sugars in large bowl until light and fluffy (about 3-5 min)
  3. Scrape sides of bowl and add in eggs one at a time on medium-low speed.
  4. Scrape sides of bowl. Mix in vanilla
  5. Add dry ingredients and mix until just combined (1-2 min).
  6. Stir in chocolate chunks.
  7. Drop 1 1/2 Tbsp onto baking sheets, leaving room for spreading.
  8. Bake cookies for 9-12 min

Wine Cookies/Ciambelle al Vino

Effort level: not too bad if you have leftover white wine, 10-40 min

Makes 1-2 dozen

Ingredients

  • 1/2 c white wine (dry)
  • 1/2 c sugar
  • 1/3 c oil
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 1/4 c ap flour
  • sugar for coating
  • 1/2-1 tsp anise seeds (optional)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350° F
  2. In a medium mixing bowl add wine, oil, sugar, salt and baking powder (add anise seeds if using) mix well, gradually add flour until it all comes together.
  3. Transfer to a slightly floured flat surface and knead lightly approximately 10 times. Dough will be soft, don't overwork it.
  4. Cut off a little dough at a time, roll into a small chubby rope (about 13 centimeters long) and bring ends together to form a circle.
  5. Dip in sugar and and place on parchment covered cookie sheets, bake for approximately 25-30 minutes, or golden color.

Ranger Cookies

Effort level: not much, 10-45 min

Makes 2-3 dozen

Ingredients

  • 1 c white sugar
  • 1 c brown sugar
  • 1 c butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • couple dashes of salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 c quick oats (or old fashioned)
  • 2 c rice crispies (or corn flakes or like cereal)
  • 255g ap flour (2 c)
  • 6 oz chocolate chips (about 2 c)

Additional add-in ideas. Measure with your heart.

  • 1/2 c chopped nuts
  • 3/4 c shredded coconut
  • 1/2 craisins
  • variation: use white chocolate chips instead with 1/2-1 c craisins
  • everything but the kitchen sink cookies: add 1/2-1 c peanut butter and any mix-ins on hand

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350° F
  2. Cream butter and sugars until light and fluffy.
  3. Add eggs one at a time on medium-low speed, scrapiing down side of bowl. Add in vanilla.
  4. Add flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, salt and stir to combine.
  5. Mix in rice crispies and chocolate chips.
  6. Bake for 10-12 min or until set on top.

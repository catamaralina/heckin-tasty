    <h2 id="pancake">IHOP Pancakes</h2>
    <p>Effort Level: Not too much.</p>
    <p>Makes 2-4 people</p>
    <h4>Ingredients</h4>
    <ul>
        <li>1 1/4 cup flour</li>
        <li>1 tsp baking powder</li>
        <li>1 tsp baking soda</li>
        <li>dash salt</li>
        <li>1 egg</li>
        <li>1 cup +2 tbsp milk</li>
        <li>2 tbsp lemon juice/white vinegar</li>
        <li>2 tbsp melted butter</li>
        <li>1/4 cup sugar</li>
        <li>optional add-ins: banana, apple, chocolate chip</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Directions</h4>
    <ol>
        <li>Mix milk and lemon juice to create buttermilk. Stir and set aside.</li>
        <li>Mix dry ingredients</li>
        <li>Stir egg and buttermilk mixture into dry ingredients.</li>
        <li>Mix in melted butter and sugar and any add-ins. Don't overmix - clumps are ok.</li>
        <li>Cook on med-high lightly greased pan.</li>
    </ol>

    <h2 id="biscuits">Sweet Blueberry Biscuits with Lemon Glaze</h2>
    <p>Effort level: yes, 30 min</p>
    <p>Makes about 10 biscuits</p>
    <h4>Ingredients</h4>
    <ul>Biscuits
        <li>2 cups all-purpose flour</li>
        <li>3 Tbsp sugar</li>
        <li>1 Tbsp baking powder</li>
        <li>1 tsp salt</li>
        <li>1/2 cup butter, frozen & shaved<li>
        <li>1 1/4 cup buttermilk (2 tbsp lemon juice with 1 cup + 2 tbsp milk)</li>
        <li>1 cup frozen blueberries (or raspberries!)</li>
        <li>1/4 cup melted butter</li>
    </ul>
    <ul>Glaze
        <li>1 cup powdered sugar</li>
        <li>2 Tbsp milk</li>
        <li>1/4 tsp vanilla extract</li>
        <li>1 Tbsp lemon juice</li>
        <li>1 tsp lemon zest (optional)</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Directions</h4>
    <ol>
        <li>Mix together flour, sugar, baking powder and salt.</li>
        <li>Add buttermilk and butter and add until incorportated. Do not overmix. Will be wet and sticky</li>
        <li>Dump onto floured surface. Work dough with hands until no longer sticky.</li>
        <li>Pat dough to 1/2 inch thickness. Scatter blueberries over dough. Fold dough in half. Fold dough in half again.</li>
        <li>Pat dough to 1 inch thickness. Use biscuit cutter to cut biscuits. Cut straight up and down, do not twist.</li>
        <li>Place biscuits into greased 9" pan, making sure they are touching.</li>
        <li>Preheat oven to 500&deg F. Bake for 8-9 minutes. Remove before they brown.</li>
        <li>Brush with melted butter then return to oven and bake until golden brown on top.</li>
        <li>GLAZE: Add all ingredients to bowl. Whisk until smooth. Drizzle over  hot biscuits.</li>
    </ol>

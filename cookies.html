Soft & Chewy Sugar Cookeis

Effort level: , min

Makes 4

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 cups (358g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (224g) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 cup + 2 tbsp (233g) granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp (28g) light brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup sugar (additional for rolling)

Directions

  1. Combine the flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt in a bowl.
  2. Cream the butter and sugars together until light in color and fluffy, 2-3 minutes. You should be able to see the change in color.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla and mix until well combined.
  4. Add the dry ingredients and mix until the dough is well combined. It will be thick and shouldn't be sticky. Do not over mix. Once it's well combined, bring it together to form a ball.
  5. Create 1 1/2 tablespoon sized balls of cookie dough. Gently roll into a ball, then roll each ball in the additional sugar to coat. Set the balls on the baking sheet.
  6. Bake cookies for 7-8 minutes at 350. The cookies will spread and the centers will look soft, but should look done. Remove just before the edges begin to turn golden. Don't over bake. The cookies will be a little puffy when you take them out of the oven but will fall a bit as they cool.

Chew Double Chocolate Cookies

Effort level: some (using scale for measuring), 15-30 min

Makes 2-3 dozen

Note: For chocolate I generally use GY 69& or 82% Van. Salt

Ingredients

  • 325 g ap flour (2 1/2 c)
  • 60 g cocoa powder (3/4 c)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 255 g unsalted butter (18 Tbsp)
  • 180 g white sugar (1 c)
  • 180 g brown sugar (1 c)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 340 g chocolate chunks (2 c)

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 350° F
  2. Cream butter and both sugars in large bowl until light and fluffy (about 3-5 min)
  3. Scrape sides of bowl and add in eggs one at a time on medium-low speed.
  4. Scrape sides of bowl. Mix in vanilla
  5. Add dry ingredients and mix until just combined (1-2 min).
  6. Stir in chocolate chunks.
  7. Drop 1 1/2 Tbsp onto baking sheets, leaving room for spreading.
  8. Bake cookies for 9-12 min

Wine Cookies/Ciambelle al Vino

Effort level: not too bad if you have leftover white wine, 10-40 min

Makes 1-2 dozen

Ingredients

  • 1/2 c white wine (dry)
  • 1/2 c sugar
  • 1/3 c oil
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 1/4 c ap flour
  • sugar for coating
  • 1/2-1 tsp anise seeds (optional)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350° F
  2. In a medium mixing bowl add wine, oil, sugar, salt and baking powder (add anise seeds if using) mix well, gradually add flour until it all comes together.
  3. Transfer to a slightly floured flat surface and knead lightly approximately 10 times. Dough will be soft, don't overwork it.
  4. Cut off a little dough at a time, roll into a small chubby rope (about 13 centimeters long) and bring ends together to form a circle.
  5. Dip in sugar and and place on parchment covered cookie sheets, bake for approximately 25-30 minutes, or golden color.

Ranger Cookies

Effort level: not much, 10-45 min

Makes 2-3 dozen

Ingredients

  • 1 c white sugar
  • 1 c brown sugar
  • 1 c butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • couple dashes of salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 c quick oats (or old fashioned)
  • 2 c rice crispies (or corn flakes or like cereal)
  • 255g ap flour (2 c)
  • 6 oz chocolate chips (about 2 c)

Additional add-in ideas. Measure with your heart.

  • 1/2 c chopped nuts
  • 3/4 c shredded coconut
  • 1/2 craisins
  • variation: use white chocolate chips instead with 1/2-1 c craisins
  • everything but the kitchen sink cookies: add 1/2-1 c peanut butter and any mix-ins on hand

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350° F
  2. Cream butter and sugars until light and fluffy.
  3. Add eggs one at a time on medium-low speed, scrapiing down side of bowl. Add in vanilla.
  4. Add flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, salt and stir to combine.
  5. Mix in rice crispies and chocolate chips.
  6. Bake for 10-12 min or until set on top.

Coconut Macaroons

Effort level: yeah, min

Makes 1-2 dozen

Note: Makes an excellent gluten free pie crust if spread out along a pie plate and baked.

Ingredients

  • 14 oz coconut flakes
  • 3/4 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 Tbsp butter (melted)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1/4 - 1/2 tsp salt
  • 12 oz dark/semisweet chocolate (OPTIONAL)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper sheets.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, stir together coconut, sweetened condensed milk, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the egg whites and salt until stiff peaks form. Once they are formed, fold egg whites into the coconut mixture, making sure to fully fold the egg whites into the coconut.
  4. Using two spoons or an ice cream scoop, scoop mounds of the coconut mixture and place on parchment paper lined baking sheets. If you don't have parchemnt paper, lightly spray with non-stick cooking spray.
  5. Bake for 20-23 minutes. Watch carefully as the bottoms can become too browned. Remove from the oven and let setup for about 2 minutes. Carefully remove and place on cooling rack.
  6. If dipping into chocolate, place the chocolate in microwave-safe bowl. Heat in 30 second increments until melted. Carefully pick up each macaroon, and dip the bottom into the melted dark chocolate. Place on a cooling rack or parchment paper lined baking sheet. Let set up and cool. Pepernoten

Effort level: low, but tedious, 30 min

Makes many

Ingredients

  • 125 g (1 c) flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 65 g (1/3 c) dark browwn sugar
  • 50 g (1/4 c) butter, room temperature
  • 1 Tbsp Speculaas spice mix
  • 1-2 Tbsp milk

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 300° F
  2. Add dry ingredients to bowl, and mix. Make a little hole in the middle.
  3. Add butter and milk into the hole in the flour mix.
  4. Start crumbling the dough with your fingers.
  5. hen dough is nice and crumbly, quickly kneed it into a supple dough.
  6. Make little balls out of the dough the size of a peanut.
  7. Place balls on a baking tray and bake the spice nuts for 15-20 min until brown and done.
